Foreword – Hold your horses: the BCB is not likely to ease so soon
The Brazilian economy is gradually rebalancing. Signs of an (mild) economic deceleration are mounting – including the labor 
market –, which suggests lower inflationary pressure in the coming quarters. The BRL has consolidated its stronger levels (at 
around 5.3 reais to the dollar), contributing to keep goods inflation under control. Inflation expectations edged down.
Globally, the Fed resumed its monetary easing cycle, and China continues to export deflation to all countries but the US (due 
to higher tariffs).
That is good news for the Brazilian Central Bank (BCB). But as we pointed out in our previous Brazil Macro Monthly, it does 
not seem to be enough to solve its problem, namely, bringing IPCA inflation to the 3.0% target (or close to it).
The economy is losing some steam, but idleness indicators such as capacity utilization rate in industry and unemployment 
rate remain at record-tight levels. And there is government stimulus in the pipeline targeting the 2026 elections – which should 
revive domestic demand.
A more aggressive Fed easing could help, but it is also unlikely. Economic activity remains solid, and inflation is stubbornly 
above target in the US – with upside risks from tariffs.
It is difficult to see the BRL strengthening much further in this scenario. Particularly considering that domestic fundamentals 
are not helping much: fiscal uncertainties are elevated, balance of payments results have deteriorated, and political/electoral 
outlook remains cloudy.
We did not change our forecasts much this month. We continue to see a “soft patch” for economic activity that will help to 
bring inflation to the target range, but not to the target. We adjusted the BRL a bit to incorporate recent appreciation (to 5.3 
from 5.5 reais per dollar for the 2025 year-end). And postponed the beginning of the monetary easing cycle by one Copom 
meeting (to March), reflecting the quite hawkish official communication from the Committee members.
Indeed, considering that the balance of risks is tilted to the upside - more fiscal stimulus, stronger domestic demand, higher 
inflation, and wider current account deficit – it is reasonable for the Copom to be more conservative, even with the baseline 
scenario evolving as expected.
We still see room for a monetary easing cycle next year, allowing a less restrictive – although still restrictive – Selic rate. Our 
terminal rate remains at 12.00%, but this forecast has an unusual degree of uncertainty, heavily dependent on the evolution 
of geopolitical risks and the perspective of fiscal reforms following next year’s elections in Brazil.

Political environment – With no defined 
opposition candidate, government focuses on 
positive agenda
Paulo Gama, Bárbara Baião, Bianca Lima e João Paulo 
Machado
In October, discussions about the opposition's candidacy for 
the Presidency are expected to remain in the spotlight, still 
under the impact of Tarcísio de Freitas' recent moves to 
distance himself from the role of protagonist in the process.
The Governor's public retreat happened weeks after his 
forceful manifestation at the opposition rally at the beginning 
of last month, on September 7th. The critical tone he adopted 
on that occasion did not bring the expected unity on the right, 
and the former Minister of Jair Bolsonaro ended up becoming 
a target both of the former President's family, in a movement 
led by Eduardo Bolsonaro, and of the Planalto Palace. The 
scenario led him to publicly state that he does not intend to be 
a candidate for the Planalto Palace next year.
Although this manifestation does not signal a definitive 
scenario — in our view, the candidacy selection process tends 
to bring him back into the game by the deadline in April —
discussions in October are expected to remain under the 
impact of this movement, with alternatives being floated in the 
news, primarily featuring the name of Ratinho Jr., Governor of 
Paraná.
At the same time, there appear to be no incentives in the 
coming weeks for Eduardo Bolsonaro to abandon the ambition 
of presenting himself as his father's successor. The 
publication of the Federal Supreme Court's (STF) ruling 
convicting Bolsonaro and the appeals that may be filed by the 
defense are likely to keep the clash with the STF in the 
spotlight. Working in the opposite direction is the movement 
toward a rapprochement between Lula and Donald Trump, 
which began with a call between the two leaders earlier this 
week. The proximity between the two leaders reduces one of 
the platforms that Eduardo was using in his favor.
As we have previously pointed out, we do not believe that Jair 
Bolsonaro's definitive decision on who should represent him in 
2026 will be announced in the short term, and this selection 
process should keep alternatives, including Tarcísio, alive.
On the government side, the focus will be on the discussion on 
expanding the Income Tax exemption bracket for those who 
earn up to BRL 5,000 a month — and the reduction for those 
earning between BRL 5,000 and BRL 7,350. The proposal is the
