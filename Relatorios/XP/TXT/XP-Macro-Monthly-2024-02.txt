Foreword – No significant changes to our forecasts, but with a short 
term positive bias
Last month, we adjusted our scenario to incorporate additional global cost-push deflation and a larger Brazilian 
trade surplus. Consequently, we reduced our IPCA inflation forecast for 2024 to 3.7% and reduced our terminal 
Selic rate forecast to 9.00%.
This has, so far, proved a correct decision. The extension of global deflation seems to be happening, although 
geopolitical conflicts – reflected in higher freight costs – are a risk. Moreover, pressure on agricultural products 
generated by El Nino is gradually dissipating. We did not change our inflation forecasts this month, but risks are 
still tilted to the downside.
Positive bias also on growth. Exports and consumption have been solid, backed by ballooning mining and oil 
production and by strong labor income growth. We will wait for 4Q23 GDP figures early next month before 
adjusting our forecasts, but our 1.50% GDP growth projection for 2024 seems to have an upside risk.
Having said that, fiscal and quasi-fiscal policy will probably remain expansionary, which, along with the tight labor 
market, tends to keep services inflation under pressure. When goods prices stabilize, headline inflation will rise. It 
may happen from 2H24 onwards.
Geopolitical tensions also tend to increase in the second half of the year, as US presidential elections approach.
Thus, despite the better short-term inflation/growth tradeoff, we do not see room for the Central Bank to step up 
the current easing pace, nor to lower the terminal Selic rate. We also see our FX projection as balanced at 4.70 
reais to the dollar at 2024 yearend.

3.25% but below the upper bound of the tolerance range 
(4.75%). The result was more than 100bps below 2022

Further disinflation in 2024. The January mid-month IPCA-
15 rose by 0.31% MoM, well below market expectations. The 
main downward surprises came from the very low variation 
in airfares and in the subitem 'licensing and vehicle plates', 
whose variation will repeat throughout the year. Industrial 
prices followed last year's well-behaved dynamics. On the 
other hand, food prices sharply rose due to the El Niño 
events, and service prices accelerated, probably reflecting 
the recent improvement in wages.  
 
As per our estimates, services inflation will not reaccelerate 
throughout the year, but if it remains at higher-than-
expected levels, headline inflation will struggle to converge 
to the 3.0% target.   
 
We keep our 2024 IPCA forecast at 3.7%.  As we mentioned 
above, the January IPCA-15 surprised us in airfares and 
license plates (-0.20 pp). However, we have raised our 
projections for other monitored goods. In the case of 
electricity, we now see higher increases at the distributors 
(+0.05 pp). In the same direction, we have removed from our 
scenario a cut in gasoline prices by Petrobras in February 
(+0.12 pp), which ended up having a direct impact on the 
price of ethanol. 
 
Industrial goods inflation is expected to remain low this 
year. We project 2.1% this year, reflecting the fall in industrial 
commodities in the last quarter of 2023, a trend that the 
January IPA-M endorsed. On the 'food at home' side, the 
dissipation of the effects of El Niño and a robust domestic 
supply of grains will continue to sustain prices at a 
comfortable level; we forecast 4.2% of food at home in 
2024.

Monetary Policy – 9.00% terminal rate, with 
balanced risks
Caio Megale
We see balanced risks for the medium-term inflation 
outlook in Brazil.
On the one hand, global cost-push disinflation continues, 
and should keep short-term CPI subdued in many regions 
and countries. Geopolitical tensions and their impact on 
freight costs are surely a risk, but so far, they are not strong 
enough to reverse goods deflation.
On the other hand, the labor market is showing mounting 
signs of overheating… The unemployment rate fell to its 
lowest level in eight years, while real earnings are on the rise. 
Labor-related items within the IPCA are showing early signs 
of additional pressure.
